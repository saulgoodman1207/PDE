In this paper, we develop two numerical methods, the Legendre-Galerkin method and the
generalized Log orthogonal functions Galerkin method for numerically solving the fully nonlinear
Monge-AmpГЁre equation. Both methods are constructed based on the vanishing moment approach.
To address both solution stability and computational eп¬ғciency, we propose a multiple-level
framework for resolving discretization schemes. The mathematical justiп¬Ғcations of the new
approaches and the error estimates for the Legendre-Galerkin method are established. Numerical
experiments validate the accuracy of our methods, and a comparative experiment demonstrates
the advantage of Log orthogonal functions for problems with corner singularities. The results
highlight that our methods have high-order accuracy and small computational cost.

The Monge-AmpГЁre equation is a well-known fully nonlinear partial diп¬Җerential equation and in a variety of broad applications,
including diп¬Җerential geometry, meteorology, cosmology, economics, and optimal mass transportation problems [4,10,18,26,35].
Due to its importance in applied science and the challenges stemming from the full nonlinearity, the convex constraint and the low
regularity of the solutions, there is a growing interest in applying eп¬ғcient and accurate numerical methods to solve this problem.
For smooth solutions of the problem (1.1), many approaches can lead to numerical methods that converge in practice [6,8,9,16,37,
38,41,46]. Such as in [17], Dean and Glowinski used mixed п¬Ғnite element method to solve the saddle-point problem, transformed by
a well-chosen augmented Lagrangian approach. Many researchers employed iterative algorithms to obtain the numerical solution of
the problem (1.1). In [39], the researchers considered some multiscale radial basis function collocation methods, such as the cascadic
meshfree method, the stationary multilevel method and the hierarchical radial basis function method, for solving the two-dimensional
Monge-AmpГЁre equation with Dirichlet boundary condition. In [5], the authors implemented the cascadic iterative algorithm and
developed the discretization of the meshless generalized п¬Ғnite diп¬Җerence method to numerically solve the problem (1.1). In [36], the
authors obtained the numerical solution by developing a trivariate spline collocation approach to solve iterative Poisson equations.
They employed an averaged algorithm to ensure the convergence of their iterations.
For weak solutions of the problem (1.1), such as viscosity and Aleksandrov solutions, numerous numerical methods have been
developed to capture and analyze these weak solutions [34]. In [42], Oliker and Prussner proposed a special discretization method
for the equation based on its geometric interpretation. This method is speciп¬Ғcally designed to approximate non-smooth Alexandrov
solutions, where the right-hand side is a sum of Dirac measures. In [27], Froese constructed a wide stencil п¬Ғnite diп¬Җerence scheme for
the Monge-AmpГЁre equation that converges to the viscosity solution. Then, Froese and Oberman [28] devised a hybrid discretization
approach for the problem (1.1), taking advantage of the optimal features of both natural discretization and monotone discretization.
In [1], Awanou oп¬Җered numerical evidence of convergence for nonsmooth solutions employing a time-marching method. In [2],
the author presented a theory explaining why standard discretizations exhibit numerical convergence for Aleksandrov solutions of
the Monge-AmpГЁre equation. In [20,22], Feng and Neilan presented the vanishing moment method to compute moment solutions,
providing an approximation to the viscosity solutions of the problem (1.1) through a quasilinear fourth-order partial diп¬Җerential
equation (PDE). Building on this concept, Feng and his collaborators developed a series of п¬Ғnite element methods in [12,19,21,
24,40]. These methods encompassed рқҗ¶ 0 penalty method, discontinuous Galerkin (DG) method, mixed п¬Ғnite element method, and
more. Recently, in [29], the researchers proposed a regularization of the Monge-AmpГЁre equation in planar convex domains through
uniformly elliptic Hamilton-Jacobi Bellman equations, eliminating the limitation imposed by convexity constraints.
However, from the literature review, it appears that there has been limited research on the application of the spectral methods for
solving the fully nonlinear Monge-AmpГЁre equation, particularly for solutions with weak singularities. Therefore, it is necessary to
develop eп¬ғcient and highly accurate numerical algorithms for these equations. In this paper, based on the vanishing moment method,
we develop the Legendre-Galerkin method with рқҗҝ2 -projection to numerically solve the fully nonlinear Monge-AmpГЁre equation (1.1).
It oп¬Җers high accuracy, and the computational cost is relatively small compared to other methods. However, eп¬Җectively managing
the singularities of equations has consistently presented a signiп¬Ғcant challenge. Inspired by Chen and Shen [13вҖ“15], we present
the GLOFs-Galerkin method for the numerical approximation of problem (1.1) with corner singularities. Considering both solution
stability and computational eп¬ғciency, we design a multiple-level framework for resolving discretization schemes. Several numerical
examples are provided to show the eп¬ғciency of our proposed methods. The main contributions and remarkable features of this study
are summarized as follows.
вҖў Based on the vanishing moment method, we propose the Legendre-Galerkin method with рқҗҝ2 -projection to numerically solve the
fully nonlinear Monge-AmpГЁre equation in two dimensional setting with the Dirichlet boundary conditions.
вҖў When dealing with singular solutions, we present the GLOFs-Galerkin method for the numerical approximation solution of
problem (1.1) with corner singularities.
вҖў To address both solution stability and computational eп¬ғciency, we propose a multiple-level framework for resolving discretization schemes.
вҖў We conduct a convergence analysis of the Legendre-Galerkin method with рқҗҝ2 -projection, discussing error estimates under рқҗ» 1 -,
рқҗ» 2 -, and рқҗҝ2 -norms, which yield optimal results.
This paper is organized as follows: In Section 2, we provide a concise introduction to the vanishing moment method and derive
its Galerkin formulation. In Section 3, based on the vanishing moment method, we establish the Legendre- and GLOFs-Galerkin
formulations for the fourth-order quasilinear equation and propose a multiple-level framework for solving discretization schemes. In
Section 4, we present the convergence analysis for the Legendre-Galerkin method. In Section 5, we provide numerical examples for
both smooth and non-smooth solutions to verify the eп¬Җectiveness of the Legendre- and GLOFs-Galerkin methods. Finally, we make
some concluding observations.

6. Concluding remarks
In this paper, we develop the Legendre-Galerkin method with рқҗҝ2 -projection and the GLOFs-Galerkin method to numerically
solve the fully nonlinear Monge-AmpГЁre equation in two dimensional setting with the Dirichlet boundary conditions. We conduct a
convergence analysis of the Legendre-Galerkin method with рқҗҝ2 -projection, discussing error estimates under рқҗ» 1 -, рқҗ» 2 -, and рқҗҝ2 -norms,
which yield optimal results. A multiple-level framework, based on the continuation method, is established to solve the discretization
schemes. Numerical experiments validate the accuracy of our approaches, with a comparative study highlighting the advantages of
log orthogonal functions in corner singularity problems. The selection of initial values for our methods is straightforward. The results
demonstrate that our methods have high-order accuracy and small computational cost. The simplicity and generality of our approaches
make it poised to attract signiп¬Ғcant interest within the science and engineering community. Given its ease of implementation, we
anticipate widespread application of our methods in diverse problem-solving scenarios.
